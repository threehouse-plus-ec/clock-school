Electronic oscillator comparison. From audio tones to RF hardware.

In Tier 0 you compared the Sun, a pendulum, and a household clock by observation and notebook. In Tier 1 you compare electronic oscillators — first in the browser, then with physical hardware.

Tier 1A — Browser Oscillator Lab

Browser App

Beat Lab

Generate two tones, observe the beat pattern, measure the frequency difference. Use internal oscillators or your device microphone with real external signals.

Browser · No install · No hardware required

Beat Lab runs entirely in the browser using the Web Audio API. It has two modes:

Internal mode: The browser generates two sine tones. You control the frequencies, hear the beat, see the waveform and spectrum, and log measurements. This is where everyone starts.

Microphone mode: The browser analyses audio from your device microphone. Feed in two tones from speakers, tuning forks, or external signal generators. The same analysis chain measures the beat frequency from real signals.

Tier 1B — External Oscillator Lab

Tier 1B uses physical electronic oscillators: a DDS (digitally synthesised frequency), a VCO (voltage-controlled oscillator), and optionally a GPS-disciplined 10 MHz reference. The Beat Lab browser interface serves as the analysis and logging front end — the same tool, now connected to real hardware.

Status: The constrained-access hardware prototype is in design. See the blueprint for the specification and bill of materials plan.

Conceptual Bridge

In Tier 0, the pendulum is free-running, the mechanical clock is regulated, and the Sun is the reference. In Tier 1, the VCO is free-running, the DDS is programmable, and the GPSDO is the reference disciplined to an atomic standard via satellite. Same logic, higher bandwidth, electronic rather than mechanical.
